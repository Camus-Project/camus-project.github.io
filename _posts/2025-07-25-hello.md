Welcome to the official Camus blog! This is where we'll share updates, insights, and developments about the Camus Project. Before diving into specifics, here's a look at the project from a global perspective.

## What is Camus?

Camus is an open initiative building a unified method, specification language, and tooling for software generated by AI but still understood and assumed by humans.

The core idea is simple: as AI writes more code, the role of the human shifts from writing every line to specifying intent, reviewing output, and taking responsibility. Camus provides the framework and tools to make that shift systematic, auditable, and certifiable.

The project is self-hosted — it develops its methods and tools by applying them to itself.

## What exists today

**Camus Method** is active and defines a three-phase framework — Lexicon (define terms), Grammar (write to rules), Certification (human signature) — for conceptualizing, documenting, and certifying AI-generated code. **Camus Skill** implements the Method as an embeddable skill for AI-assisted workflows; it is under active development.

## What is in development

**Camus SL** will be an embeddable specification language to express intent in a form understandable by both humans and AI. **kiss** will be a companion CLI tool for validation, generation, and certification. **Camus PL** remains a long-term objective — a programming language focused on readability, explicit semantics, and long-term maintainability. Each component will be developed when the Method and SL reach sufficient maturity.
